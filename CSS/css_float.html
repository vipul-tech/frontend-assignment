Nalanda (Nālandā, pronounced [naːlən̪d̪aː]) was a renowned Hindu and Buddhist university in ancient Magadha (modern-day Bihar), India.[6][7] Considered by historians to be the world's very first residential university,[8] and among the greatest centers of learning in the ancient world, it was located near the city of Rajagriha (now Rajgir) and about 90 kilometres (56 mi) southeast of Pataliputra (now Patna),operating from 427 to 1197 CE.[9][10] Nalanda played a vital role in promoting the patronage of arts and academics during the 5th and 6th century CE, a period that has since been described as the "Golden Age of India" by scholars.
